About Me

Derek Xu

Hi! I am a curious and diligent senior at Dublin High School's Engineering Academy. After I graduate, I want to find a job in the field of engineering or computer science. To achieve this goal, I am taking challenging science, engineering, and mathematics courses, and I am working toward a major in computer science.

My passion for technology and its development makes me interested in engineering. For instance, I love exploring new products from companies like Google and Apple. I enjoy using many of their products, such as the iPhone and Google Drive. I admire their cutting-edge technology and quick innovations.

One of my biggest accomplishments in high school is learning about engineering as both a discipline and a potential career. In addition to becoming competent with Python and Java, I have also had hands-on learning experiences and projects in mechanical and electrical engineering in high school engineering classes. For example, I have worked with peers to create a Python version of Hangman, an educational game in Java, a mechanical winch, and solar and hydrogen-powered vehicles. Furthermore, I explored careers in engineering by listening to guest speakers who specialize in various fields from artificial intelligence to civil engineering. In addition to attending guest talks, I participated in both in-person and virtual field trips to immerse myself in both careers and research related to engineering. I have seen the National Ignition Facility at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory with my own eyes and listened to professionals who have worked at companies such as Microsoft and Amazon. I have greatly enjoyed learning about different forms of engineering and engineering careers with my teachers and industry professionals.

Experiences outside of the Engineering Academy have also enhanced my knowledge of engineering. During freshman year, I was in the academy of the Gael Force Robotics club, where I learned about robot design and mechanical engineering and built a VEX competition robot prototype with other students. In the summer after junior year, I participated in UC Santa Barbara's Summer Research Academies program, where I learned about machine learning and developed neural networks. With a team, I completed a research project utilizing neural networks to determine the effectiveness of COVID-19 vaccines. In this project, we concluded that COVID-19 vaccines were effective, and the application of neural networks helped broaden our knowledge of computer science and software engineering.

Through experiences inside and outside of the Engineering Academy, I discovered my strong interest in technology, and hope to pursue a career in this area. Thank you for reading this page, and I invite you to check out other areas of this page, using the links at the top of the page.
