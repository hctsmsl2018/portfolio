Digital Electronics Honors


Random Number Generator

2 weeks from January 13, 2021 to January 24, 2021

For this project, I analyzed the outputs of various parts of a random number generator circuit and combined them in the Multisim circuit design software individually. The random number generator outputs a random number from 1 to 6, which are shown with arrangements of dots identical to those found on a 6-sided die. I learned how the timers in a circuit can aid the generation of random numbers and how a circuit signal with a high frequency and a switch can be used to generate random numbers, and how combinational and sequential components of circuits can be combined with each other. One of the challenges I faced in this project was troubleshooting the circuit such that it can produce the correct output. Through comparing my circuit with the circuit diagram provided by my teacher, I was able to find the mistakes in my circuit and fix them so that it will produce the correct output. Another challenge was figuring out where to put a switch so that the circuit will stop running at a random number. I was able to determine that the switch needs to be placed between the clock and the sequential logic part of the circuit, as toggling it will disconnect or connect the signal from the clock, which governs whether the circuit is stopped at a certain value or still running.

Binary Counter

3 weeks from November 8, 2021 to November 29, 2021

In this project, I first analyzed a binary counter circuit and then designed a binary counter circuit physically from a circuit diagram individually. First, I analyzed the circuit’s output pattern, period, and frequency of the outputs, which reflect the binary nature of the circuit’s output. After analyzing the circuit on the Multisim circuit design software, I created the circuit on a breadboard and created a recording of its output. During this project, I learned how to make a circuit’s output clearer by slowing down the frequency at which it counts at and constructing complex circuits from diagrams or instructions. I also learned how binary can be used to create counters that only involve lights which can be either on or off. The primary challenge I faced in this project was trying to recreate the circuit from the circuit diagram and getting it to work. I overcame this challenge by utilizing an instructional video to create a part of the circuit created by my teacher along with diagrams of the final circuit. To troubleshoot the circuit after I finished it, I double checked the circuit with the circuit diagrams and the teacher’s directions. After I finished my circuit, I also helped four other classmates troubleshoot their circuits and successfully fix them based on photos of the final circuit and the teacher’s video.

I was able to overcome this challenge by understanding the requirements of the program I needed to create and figure out how to translate them into different tasks. After my partner and I finalized our program, we presented it to another pair of students who evaluated how well our code works and if it produces the intended results, and my partner and I evaluated their code. Renewable Insulation Project

2 weeks from February 4, 2020 to February 19, 2020

In this project, I designed an insulation material with a team of three other students. The insulation material had to be made out of completely renewable and biodegradable materials, such as wood, paper, and clay. We researched each material we used to better understand certain factors affecting their usefulness, such as its environmental impact and cost-effectiveness. On our project documentation, we described each material based on our research. One of my main roles in the project was to contribute a layer of magazine paper to our insulation material. For the magazine paper, I took several old magazines and cut them to a size that corresponds with that of other parts of our insulation. I then added a clay border around the sheets of magazine paper used for the insulation, so that the magazine paper stays together. Another major contribution I made in this project was performing calculations for values that describe the insulator's effectiveness. For example, using experimental data gathered when testing the insulator, I calculated the thermal conductivity constant, U-value, and R-value for my insulator. When I showed my calculations for my group to check, I made sure to see if everyone agreed and solve disputes if necessary. Even though everyone else agreed with another teammate's work, I found an error and was able to work with my team to correct it. One of my challenges during this project was being neat and exact in my cutting of the magazine paper. When I tried cutting the magazine paper using a paper cutter, the paper cutter sometimes displaced the paper rather than cutting through it, even though I tried to cut fewer sheets of paper at a time. Still, to preserve the exactness of my cutting of the paper, I made shorter cuts at a time to better preserve the exactness. Whenever the paper cutter can not cut through the paper without displacing it, I used a pocket knife to cut the paper to prevent displacing it.

Mechanical Winch Project

2 weeks from October 14, 2019 to October 23, 2019

For this project, three other students and I worked on a physical model of a mechanical winch and a program using NetLogo that models it in terms of the behavior of the real-life winch. Also, a documentation describing the winch and program's functionality and development were required. The physical winch needs to have the ability to lift a mass of 100 grams for 30 centimeters. It also needs to be made of only VEX of FischerTech parts, and be powered by a FischerTech motor. The simulation of the winch must show how the size of the surface the rope of the winch coils around and the voltage and current of the electrical power of the winch affects the rate and angular speed at which the cable of the winch coils. During this project, I mainly worked on the program that simulates the behavior of the winch, while I also worked a little bit on the physical prototype of the winch. I worked on all parts of the simulations, such as all of the variables affecting the speed at which the winch works on and the background for the winch model in the simulation. On the project documentation, I included pictures of the code and interface for the program. I also made sure the project documentation is neat and consistent after everyone finished their parts.
